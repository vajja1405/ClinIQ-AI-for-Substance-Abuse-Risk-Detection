Introduction & Motivation

Substance Use Disorder (SUD) is vastly undercoded in hospital settings. When acute admissions (e.g., pneumonia, endocarditis) fail to document underlying SUD dependencies, hospitals face extreme Medicare DRG severity downgrades, and the patient vanishes from CDC epidemiological surveillance.

Research Question: Can large language models coupled with Retrieval-Augmented Generation (RAG) accurately detect hidden SUD signals in patient-written social text, and map those exact behaviors to missed clinical hospital billing codes?

Dataset & Methodology

We analyze 52,184 anonymized drug reviews (Kaggle 2018) paired with real, auditable government resources embedded tightly into a high-dimensional PostgreSQL vector database (`pgvector`), utilizing the `all-MiniLM-L6-v2` architecture.

  • Social Signals: 3,316 filtered SUD-relevant patient reviews.
  • RAG Targets: Baseline CMS MedPAR Data, CDC FY24 IPPS DRG Tables, ICD-10-CM Coding Guidelines.

Model Comparisons (Task 1)

We evaluated three discrete detection frameworks on 600 validation records tracking specific SUD distress markers:

  • Rule-Based: F1 = 0.859 | High precision baseline.
  • Cosine Similarity (Embedding): F1 = 0.670 | Maximized recall screening.
  • LLM+RAG (Claude): F1 = 0.477 (Precision: 88.3%) | Conservative, absolute precision needed for clinical inference.

Clinical Documentation Bridge (Results)

Connecting the phenomenological signals back to the financial reality, we simulated the pipeline across a 105-record synthetic claim hospital database. The Claude RAG AI agent scanned the raw patient admission notes and seamlessly compared them against official CMS documentation.

Conclusion & Future Work

  • Conclusion: ClinIQ successfully establishes a closed-loop socio-technical framework; utilizing social text as a leading surveillance indicator and dynamically executing clinical billing gap predictions to secure funding and epidemiological visibility.
  • Ethical Alignment: RAG limits catastrophic AI hallucinations by anchoring inferences entirely to verifiable CMS/CDC data lines.
  • Future Work: Integrate the Streamlit framework natively alongside HL7/FHIR EHR protocols at the point-of-care.
